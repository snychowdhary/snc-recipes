Masala Chai

Description

Masala chai is a tea beverage made by boiling black tea in milk and water with a mixture of aromatic herbs and spices. Originating in India, the beverage has gained worldwide popularity, becoming a feature in many coffee and tea houses.

Ingredients (Servings: 2)

  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 (1/2 inch) piece fresh ginger, chopped
  • 4 whole cloves
  • ½ cinnamon stick
  • 5 pods green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 pod black cardamom, split open
  • 2 black tea bags
  • ⅔ cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar

Steps

  1. Bring water to a boil in a small pot. Add ginger, cloves, cinnamon stick, green cardamom, and black cardamom.
  2. Simmer until flavors infuse, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add tea bags; simmer for 1 to 2 minutes.
  4. Add milk and sugar; bring back to a boil.
  5. Strain tea into mugs.
